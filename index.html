Reciply - Recipe Collection Vision Statement

The Reciply app is a user-friendly mobile recipe management tool that provides centralized recipe storage, meal planning tools, cooking instructions and sharing capabilities to simplify and enhance the cooking experience.

This app will be used by home cooks, culinary enthusiasts, families, fitness enthusiasts, and individuals learning to cook who need a simple and effective way to organize, access, and share recipes. Unlike traditional handwritten recipe books or scattered digital files, Reciply will offer an intuitive, all-in-one platform with exciting features such as the ability to create recipes, shopping list generation, and tools to support dietary goals and cooking skill development.

Reciply will be designed to allow people to store and share their recipes, plan their meals, and discover new culinary ideas using recipes shared by others. The app will be designed to be simple and accessible to allow users to perform many tasks easily, whether it be figuring out what to cook, creating a shopping list, or designing recipes they would like to share with others. It can also be used to inspire creativity in the kitchen, helping users experiment with new flavors while keeping track of the meals they love. Reciply will allow users to be able to access recipes anytime, whether at home, at the store, or on the go, making it a true companion in the kitchen.

Reciply will be valuable to users because it makes cooking easier and more enjoyable. It will take the stress out of deciding what to cook by offering instant access to recipes and shopping lists. It will also be a treasure chest for experimenting with new ideas and keeping track of your favorite creations. Users will be able to preserve cherished family recipes, keeping traditions alive for generations. Users will also be able to stay on track with tools that help plan nutritious meals. And for those just starting their cooking journey, Reciply makes it simple and fun with guided recipes and helpful tips and tutorials. By taking care of the messy, time-consuming parts of cooking, Reciply will allow users to focus on the joy of cooking by taking care of the organizational hassles, ultimately enhancing their culinary experience. It will create value for food lovers by fostering inspiration, saving time, and connecting people through the shared love of food.

For Reciply to succeed, it must achieve three key milestones. First, the app should reach at least 7,000 downloads within the first three months, showing strong interest and adoption from users. Second, it should maintain an average rating of 3.5 or higher, reflecting user satisfaction and trust in its features. Lastly, at least 40% of users should actively engage with the app weekly, proving its value as a dependable and enjoyable cooking companion. These goals will help ensure Reciply becomes a cherished tool for home cooks, families, and food lovers everywhere.

Intended Users

Reciply is crafted for a diverse community of food enthusiasts, from busy home cooks and families preserving treasured recipes to adventurous culinary explorers and health-conscious individuals managing dietary needs. Whether you're an experienced chef or a beginner learning the ropes, Reciply provides an intuitive, inspiring platform tailored to every cooking journey.

Overview of Major Functionality

Reciply delivers a robust suite of features designed to streamline and enhance your cooking experience:

  • User Registration & Login: Secure, personalized access to manage your culinary profile.
  • Recipe Management: Create, edit, view, and delete recipes for seamless organization and storage.
  • Meal Planner: Schedule your weekly meals and effortlessly plan your culinary calendar.
  • Shopping List Generation: Automatically generate shopping lists based on your selected recipes.
  • Recipe Search: Easily search for existing recipes using powerful search functionality.
  • Guided Cooking Instructions: Follow detailed, step-by-step instructions to bring your recipes to life.

Postmortem & Retrospective

Below is a reflection on our development process, addressing key questions:

1. What Went Right

The development process of our Reciply app was a collaborative effort that resulted in several successful outcomes. Through thorough planning and careful implementation of key features, we were able to deliver a functional and scalable application.

Application Structure and Planning

  • One of the things that contributed to our success was our application structure and careful planning. From the beginning, we chose to use the 3-layer architecture consisting of the presentation, business, and persistence layers. This approach enabled us to split the tasks evenly within our group and take responsibility for each of the layers assigned to them which effectively maintains a clear codebase and a clear understanding of each of our responsibilities. Furthermore, carefully breaking down features into smaller ones such as user stories, and breaking it down further into developer tasks, has significantly impacted our task management in a more efficient and positive way. This led to a comprehensive understanding of individual tasks to be given out that does not lead to developer task conflicts as this is one of the main problems that arise during a professional software development cycle. Each of the developers knows which layer they’re working on and knows what tasks to do, which leads to a smooth development process without any code conflicts.

Testing and Debugging

  • Additionally, one of the crucial aspects of our development process was our commitment to deliver working and testable features. Early on in our development process, we utilized JUnit for unit testing to make sure that individual classes, and even the methods, work perfectly and seamlessly without its dependencies and to verify their correctness. This led to us catching bugs and fatal errors before it gets implemented into our main branch. Furthermore, as our project continues forward and scaled considerably, we began to utilize integration testing to further test each of the classes and methods and verify its correctness between the seams of layers, especially the logic-to-persistence layer. This improved our confidence that methods that are used are working seamlessly across each of the layers. Finally, we used Espresso to test our presentation layer and do system/acceptance tests to verify that a feature is working as intended.Debugging was also used for the things that we could not really anticipate in testing which allowed us to monitor and comprehensively track where things go wrong. Through these tests, we have resolved major issues before they even get finalized, ensuring the application’s reliability and minimizing unexpected fatal errors and issues.

Scalable Database Implementation

  • Lastly, another contributor to the success of our application is the scalable implementation of our persistence layer. Ensuring that this layer remains scalable is one of the most crucial aspects of our application as it grows and begins to implement more features. Initially, we began to implement our persistence layer which uses only stubs, however, knowing that we would scale into a working database, we carefully ensured that it will be easy to implement the HSQLDB implementation and in the future, if we use another database, it is easily implemented through the use of interfaces. DSO (Domain-specific objects) models were also thoroughly implemented to be scalable. This ensures that if we begin to add more attributes to an object, such as a recipe’s image, date added, etc., they would be easily implemented through the use of an appropriate design pattern, such as what we did with our Recipe object using a Builder design pattern . This allows for flexibility in the future without greatly refactoring our application as not only it provided clear data management, but also made it easier to extend functionality.
  • In conclusion, our structured approach to development, careful testing and debugging, and a scalable and maintainable persistence layer were some of the notable aspects that contributed to our Reciply app’s success. By doing these approaches, we created a functional, stable, and scalable application which provides a strong foundation for future enhancements and expansion.

2. Design Smells and Brilliant Decisions

Throughout our development of this application, we have utilized some strong design choices that enhanced the maintainability and extensibility of our application. However there also some code smells that, if left unaddressed, could hinder the development process in the future as this develops further.

Brilliant Design Decisions

    One of the most important and clever aspects of our development process was the use of design patterns, which improves scalability, flexibility, and maintainability. Here are some of the notable design patterns that were used throughout our development process:

    Effective use of design patterns

    • Adapter Pattern (UI Components):
      • These are used to handle dynamic UI elements such as the RecyclerView from our main recipes homepage.
    • Builder Pattern (for Recipe DSO Model):
      • Since this is one of the most important objects in our application, this would be subjected to constant changes and additionally key features that would be implemented
      • This design pattern effectively allows for easily adding/deleting attributes for the recipe object without the extensive use of constructor overloading.
    • Singleton Pattern (Persistence Objects Instances):
      • To manage database interactions more efficiently
      • This prevents unnecessary multiple database connections and ensures consistent data access across all layers of the application.

Code Smells (To be improved in the future)

  • Lack of Code Documentation:

    Perhaps one noticeable issue in our codebase is that some of the classes, and even methods, lack necessary comments and documentation. This may raise concerns in the future as it would be difficult to read and follow the codebase if perhaps another team would eventually handle this project. There were some instances where:

    • Complex methods, such as in the business layer, lack necessary step-through comments to ensure that other developers reading may follow along the logic.
    • Responsibilities of classes are not documented properly, which would lead to unnecessary code being added in the future that may violate the Single-responsibility principle, and other SOLID principles.
  • Hardcoded Strings:

    Another design issue was the excessive use of hardcoded strings, especially in the presentation and business layer. Instead of using string resources that are particularly designed for these, such as the strings.xml, some parts contains:

    • UI texts are hardcoded, making it difficult to be maintained and internationalized.
    • Some error messages are hardcoded, instead of making them constant in a file (e.g. strings.xml)
  • Presence of Unused Code:

    While this perhaps may help in the future such that developers may not need to implement anymore, saving time, this could lead to several issues such as creating confusion among future developers. Regularly refactoring and removing dead code ensures that the code is clean and easily maintained, using only necessary code that would be used.
