Reciply - Recipe Collection Vision Statement

The Reciply app is a user-friendly mobile recipe management tool that provides centralized recipe storage, meal planning tools, cooking instructions and sharing capabilities to simplify and enhance the cooking experience.

This app will be used by home cooks, culinary enthusiasts, families, fitness enthusiasts, and individuals learning to cook who need a simple and effective way to organize, access, and share recipes. Unlike traditional handwritten recipe books or scattered digital files, Reciply will offer an intuitive, all-in-one platform with exciting features such as the ability to create recipes, shopping list generation, and tools to support dietary goals and cooking skill development.

Reciply will be designed to allow people to store and share their recipes, plan their meals, and discover new culinary ideas using recipes shared by others. The app will be designed to be simple and accessible to allow users to perform many tasks easily, whether it be figuring out what to cook, creating a shopping list, or designing recipes they would like to share with others. It can also be used to inspire creativity in the kitchen, helping users experiment with new flavors while keeping track of the meals they love. Reciply will allow users to be able to access recipes anytime, whether at home, at the store, or on the go, making it a true companion in the kitchen.

Reciply will be valuable to users because it makes cooking easier and more enjoyable. It will take the stress out of deciding what to cook by offering instant access to recipes and shopping lists. It will also be a treasure chest for experimenting with new ideas and keeping track of your favorite creations. Users will be able to preserve cherished family recipes, keeping traditions alive for generations. Users will also be able to stay on track with tools that help plan nutritious meals. And for those just starting their cooking journey, Reciply makes it simple and fun with guided recipes and helpful tips and tutorials. By taking care of the messy, time-consuming parts of cooking, Reciply will allow users to focus on the joy of cooking by taking care of the organizational hassles, ultimately enhancing their culinary experience. It will create value for food lovers by fostering inspiration, saving time, and connecting people through the shared love of food.

For Reciply to succeed, it must achieve three key milestones. First, the app should reach at least 7,000 downloads within the first three months, showing strong interest and adoption from users. Second, it should maintain an average rating of 3.5 or higher, reflecting user satisfaction and trust in its features. Lastly, at least 40% of users should actively engage with the app weekly, proving its value as a dependable and enjoyable cooking companion. These goals will help ensure Reciply becomes a cherished tool for home cooks, families, and food lovers everywhere.

Intended Users

Reciply is designed for food enthusiasts of all skill levels — from home cooks looking for everyday inspiration to culinary adventurers eager to try global cuisines. Whether you're meal prepping, managing dietary preferences, or just exploring new ideas, Reciply supports your cooking journey.

Overview of Major Functionality

The app allows users to browse curated recipe collections, save personal favorites, customize dietary filters, and share dishes with friends. Additional features include step-by-step instructions, ingredient substitution tips, and the ability to upload and organize personal recipes.

Postmortem & Retrospective

Below is a reflection on our development process, addressing key questions:

  • Overall Architecture: We adopted a layered architecture separating the front-end (using a modern UI framework) from the back-end (using RESTful APIs and a database). This approach allowed each layer to be developed and tested independently.
  • What Went Right: Agile practices (daily stand-ups, scrums, pair programming) enabled quick identification of issues and faster iteration. Frequent communication helped us meet deadlines and stay on track with our user stories.
  • What Went Wrong: Early on, merging conflicts and inconsistent coding styles caused delays. We resolved this by implementing a strict branching strategy and style guidelines, but it cost us valuable time.
  • What We Would Do Differently: We would invest more time in design and prototyping before coding. Setting up robust continuous integration testing earlier would also reduce bugs slipping through the cracks.
  • Project Size & Quantifiables: The project includes about 50 methods and 18 classes in total. Around 40% are devoted to UI and front-end functionality, 40% to back-end logic and data handling, and 20% to testing and utility classes. Each team member contributed approximately 15–20 hours per week.
  • Time Spent & Surprises: UI polishing took the most time due to frequent user feedback and design tweaks. Implementing the recipe search feature was surprisingly quick once the back-end was structured properly.
  • Design Smells or Brilliant Decisions: Our decision to keep data models lightweight and flexible turned out great for quickly iterating on new features. However, we occasionally found ourselves duplicating validation logic between front-end and back-end, which we consider a minor design smell.
  • Outstanding Bugs: We have a rare edge case where certain special characters in recipe names cause display issues. We’re planning a patch to address it in a future release.
  • Features That Exceeded Expectations: The user-generated shopping list feature quickly became a favorite, garnering positive feedback for its ease of use and time-saving capabilities.
  • Extra Technologies Used: We integrated a cloud-based testing service for device simulation, which wasn't strictly required but helped expedite QA on multiple platforms.
  • Specific Techniques: We practiced TDD for core functionalities, conducted pair programming for complex features, and held scrums twice a week to maintain alignment.
  • Project Changes from Iteration 0: Our initial vision was mostly maintained, though we added user profile features based on feedback and shifted some priorities (e.g., the shopping list tool rose to higher importance).
  • Lessons Learned About Team Development: We learned that transparent communication and frequent code reviews are crucial. Next time, we'll start with stronger style guidelines, keep daily stand-ups short but frequent, and maintain an updated product backlog to avoid scope creep.
  • Conclusions: Building Reciply confirmed the importance of an iterative approach, robust testing, and strong teamwork. Focusing on the user experience from the start proved vital for delivering a product that genuinely simplifies cooking and recipe management.
